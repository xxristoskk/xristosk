About Me

Hello! My name is Xristos, and I am a problem explorer and solver interested in the intersections of philosophy and data. One might ask "How does philosophy relate to data"? Numbers on their own don't have any meaning, and poor interpretations of them can result in loss of money, time, and/or the spreading of misinformation.

I studied philosophy and biology at Harold Washington college, continued philosophy at Brooklyn college, and went through Flatiron's Data Science Immersive Bootcamp. You can find more details about me on my blog.

Currently, I am open to roles involving research, data analysis, natural language processing, and project management.
